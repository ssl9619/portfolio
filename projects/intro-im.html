Course Overview

Introduction to Interactive Media at NYUAD was a semester-long course exploring physical computing, creative coding, and the intersection of technology with human experience. The work spanned Arduino hardware, p5.js sketches, serial communication, and iterative design — culminating in a fully interactive final project.

The course pushed me to think beyond screens: to design experiences that feel physical, have real stakes, and invite people in. The final project — Safe Russian Roulette — brought all of that together.

Course Work Includes

  • Creative coding sketches in p5.js
  • Physical switch controlled by ear movement (glasses + wire contact)
  • Collaborative Arduino drum pad with recording & playback
  • Midterm: Mexican Standoff — two-player reaction game in p5.js
  • Final: Safe Russian Roulette — Arduino + p5.js physical game

Final Project — Safe Russian Roulette

The premise: two players face each other across a table. A virtual revolver is loaded with a mix of live rounds and blanks. Each player can choose to shoot themselves (strategic — if it's blank, they keep the turn and raise the odds) or shoot the other player. The player who takes a live round loses a life, and the loser of the match has to use a shock pen — the real-world stake that makes every decision matter.

The hardware consists of four buttons (two per player), a potentiometer for menu navigation, and two LEDs — all connected to an Arduino. The Arduino and the p5.js sketch communicate in real-time over serial: button presses trigger game events, and the sketch sends a handshake back.

The p5.js sketch manages everything on screen: a scene management class controls the flow between start menu, difficulty selection, gameplay, and end screen. Difficulty levels range from Standard (one live round) to Masochist (three to five live rounds). Each player has three lives.

Key Technical Contributions

  • Built scene management system in p5.js across multiple files
  • Implemented full serial communication between Arduino and p5.js
  • Designed five-tier difficulty system with randomised live rounds
  • Wired and soldered 4 buttons, potentiometer, and 2 LEDs on custom breadboard
  • Held-button UI interaction for tactile, satisfying navigation
