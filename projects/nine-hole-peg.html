Project Overview

A VR rehabilitation simulation for stroke and MS patients undergoing dexterity training. Built in Unity (C#), it translates the standard clinical Nine Hole Peg Test into an immersive, adaptive experience with real-time hand tracking and haptic feedback.

The core innovation is an AI-driven difficulty model that personalises task complexity based on each patient's live performance metrics — meeting each patient where they are, session by session.

The MVP was deployed in active clinical settings at NYUAD. Hand tracking was grounded in a comprehensive literature review of motor rehabilitation best practices, and therapist feedback drove iterative improvements throughout.

Key Contributions

  • Built VR simulation with hand tracking and haptic feedback in Unity / C#
  • Implemented AI-driven difficulty adaptation based on patient performance
  • Grounded hand feature tracking in motor rehabilitation literature review
  • Deployed MVP in clinical settings and iterated on therapist feedback
  • Co-built working product as part of 2-person team
