Project Overview

Threes is a mobile puzzle game played on a 4×4 grid. Tiles numbered 1 and 2 merge together to form 3, and from 3 onward matching tiles merge (3+3=6, 6+6=12, and so on). The board fills up with each move, and the goal is to build the highest-value tile possible before running out of space. It sounds simple — and it is to learn, but notoriously difficult to master.

This project involved building and training a Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) agent to play Threes autonomously. The agent was trained over 500,000 episodes and achieved a best score of 2670. The training plot shows the raw episode scores, moving average, running maximum, and 90th-percentile windowed performance — all converging to a stable plateau, with occasional breakout games reaching the peak.

Several other algorithms were explored along the way — including DQN, A2C, and various hybrids — but PPO consistently outperformed them. There is meaningful room for further improvement: better reward shaping, curriculum learning, and more sophisticated state representations are all avenues worth exploring.

Key Contributions

  • Implemented and trained a PPO agent from scratch to play Threes
  • Evaluated and compared multiple RL algorithms (DQN, A2C, PPO, hybrids)
  • Achieved a best score of 2670 over 500,000 training episodes
  • Designed reward shaping and state representation for the tile-merging environment
  • Analysed training stability via moving averages, running max, and percentile tracking
